#### Privacy Policy for CyberAlerts

**Last updated:** 14/07/2026

##### 1. Introduction

CyberAlerts ("we", "us", "our") is a cybersecurity and scam awareness app for local communities. This policy explains what personal data we collect, why we collect it, and how we handle it in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

If you have any questions about this policy, you can contact us at cyberfocus@code-prem.dev.

##### 2. Who We Are

CyberAlerts is developed by Przemyslaw Kaniewski and Folayo Aina on behalf of the Cyberfocus grant. We act as the data controller for the personal data described in this policy.

##### 3. What Data We Collect

We collect the following categories of data:

**Account information**
- Email address
- Display name (if provided)
- Authentication identifiers (via Firebase Authentication)
- Currently, sign-in is available via email and password, or Google Sign-In. We may add further sign-in methods (such as other social or single sign-on providers) in future. Regardless of method, passwords are stored and managed securely by Firebase Authentication using industry-standard hashing; we never see or store your password in plain text, and for social sign-in options we do not receive your password at all

**Usage and analytics data**
- Features used within the app (e.g. quizzes completed, alerts viewed, tools accessed)
- Daily challenge activity, streaks, and badge progress
- App interaction data (e.g. session length, crash reports, general usage patterns)
- Device type and operating system version, for the purpose of app performance and compatibility

We do not knowingly collect any special category data (such as health, religious, or biometric data).

##### 4. How We Use Your Data

We use your data to:
- Create and manage your account
- Sync your progress (quizzes, badges, streaks) across devices
- Send you relevant alerts and notifications, where you have opted in
- Understand how the app is used so we can improve it
- Maintain the security and integrity of the app
- Respond to support requests

##### 5. Legal Basis for Processing

Under UK GDPR, we rely on the following legal bases:
- **Contract**: to provide the core features of the app once you create an account
- **Consent**: for optional features such as push notifications, which you can withdraw at any time
- **Legitimate interests**: for basic analytics and app improvement, in a way that does not override your rights

##### 6. Data Sharing and Third Parties

CyberAlerts uses Firebase (provided by Google) for authentication, and may also use other Firebase services (such as Firebase Analytics or Firebase Cloud Messaging for push notifications) as the app develops. Firebase is our sole third-party data processor at this time.

Firebase may process data outside the UK. Where this happens, Google provides appropriate safeguards, such as Standard Contractual Clauses or an adequacy decision, as set out in Google's own privacy and data processing terms.

We do not sell your personal data to third parties.

##### 7. Data Storage and Security

Account and usage data is stored on our backend infrastructure using industry-standard security practices, including encrypted connections and access controls. While we take reasonable steps to protect your data, no method of transmission or storage is 100% secure.

##### 8. Data Retention

We retain your personal data for as long as your account is active. If you delete your account, we will delete your stored data, except where we are required to retain it for legal reasons.

##### 9. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:
- Access the personal data we hold about you
- Request correction of inaccurate data
- Request deletion of your data
- Object to or restrict certain processing
- Request a copy of your data in a portable format
- Withdraw consent at any time, where processing is based on consent

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at cyberfocus@code-prem.dev. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.

##### 10. Children's Privacy

CyberAlerts is not intended for children under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children below this age. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us so we can remove it.

##### 11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted here with an updated "Last updated" date. Significant changes will be communicated within the app where appropriate.
